    A pancake, also known as a hotcake, griddlecake, or flapjack, is a flat type of batter bread like cake, often thin and round, prepared from a starch-based batter that may contain eggs, milk, and butter, and then cooked on a hot surface such as a griddle or frying pan.

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 3 ½ teaspoons baking powder
  • 3 ½ teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 ¼ cups milk
  • 3 tablespoons melted butter (plus extra for the pan) or vegetable oil
  • Optional: 2 tablespoons sugar, vanilla extract

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Combine Dry Ingredients In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, salt, and optional sugar.
  2. Mix Wet Ingredients In a separate medium bowl, whisk the egg well, then stir in the milk and melted butter (or oil).
  3. Combine Wet and Dry Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients. Mix until just combined; a few small lumps are fine. Do not overmix the batter, as this will result in tough, dense pancakes. Let the batter rest for about 5-10 minutes to allow the leaveners to work.
  4. Heat the Skillet Preheat a large nonstick skillet or griddle over medium heat. Lightly brush it with a small amount of melted butter or oil. You can test the temperature by sprinkling a few drops of water; if the droplets "dance" and sizzle, the pan is ready.
  5. Cook the Pancakes Pour about 1/4 cup of batter per pancake onto the hot skillet.
  6. Flip Cook for 1 to 2 minutes, or until bubbles appear on the surface and the edges look dry and set. Carefully flip the pancakes with a spatula
  7. Finish Cooking Cook the other side for another 1 to 2 minutes, or until golden brown and cooked through. Avoid pressing down on the pancakes with the spatula.
  8. Serve Transfer the cooked pancakes to a plate. You can keep early batches warm in a 200°F (93°C) oven while you finish the rest.
